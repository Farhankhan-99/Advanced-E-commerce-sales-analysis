# E-commerce Advanced Sales Analysis
Advanced e-commerce sales analysis using SQL and Power BI to drive data-driven business decisions. This project leverages PostgreSQL for comprehensive data exploration and transformation, combined with interactive Power BI dashboards to uncover actionable insights on customer segmentation, product performance, and revenue optimization.

### **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**

**1. Database Exploration**:Explore all database objects, tables, columns, and schema structure to understand data architecture

**2. Dimension Exploration**
- Identify all unique countries from which customers originate and analyze complete product category hierarchy

**3. Date Exploration**
- Determine the timeframe of available sales data including first and last order dates, and identify the youngest and oldest customers in the database

**4. Measures Exploration**
- Calculate key business metrics including total sales revenue, total items sold, average selling price, total orders, total products, total customers, and active customers who placed orders

**5. Magnitude Analysis**
- Analyze customer distribution by country and gender, product distribution by category, average cost per category, total revenue by category, revenue generated by each customer, and distribution of sold items across countries

**6. Ranking Analysis**
- Identify top 5 revenue-generating products, bottom 5 worst-performing products, top 10 customers by revenue, and top 3 customers with fewest orders placed

### **Advanced Data Analysis**

**1. Changes Over Time**
- Analyze sales performance trends over time including monthly and yearly variations in total sales, customer counts, and quantities sold

**2. Cumulative Analysis**
- Calculate total sales per year with running totals and moving average prices to identify cumulative growth patterns

**3. Performance Analysis**
- Compare each product's current year sales against its historical average performance and previous year sales to identify growth or decline trends

**4. Proportional Analysis**
- Determine which product categories contribute the most to overall sales revenue with percentage contribution metrics

**5. Data Segmentation**
- Segment products into cost ranges (Below $100, $100-$500, $500-$1000, Above $1000) and count product distribution across segments

**6. Customer Segmentation & Behavioral Analysis**
- Classify customers into three segments: VIP (12+ months history, >$5000 spend), Regular (12+ months history, â‰¤$5000 spend), and New (<12 months)

**7. Customer Report View**
- Consolidate customer metrics including age groups, segments, total orders, total sales, lifespan, recency, average order value, and average monthly spending

**8. Product Report View**
- Aggregate product metrics including revenue segments (High-Performers >$50K, Mid-Range $10K-$50K, Low-Performers <$10K), total orders, quantities sold, customer reach, lifespan, recency, average order revenue, and monthly revenue patterns
